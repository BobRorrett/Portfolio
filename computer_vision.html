Controlling a rolling robot using computer vision and path planning

This project involved building a fully autonomous rolling robot from scratch. In a team of three, I was responsible for creating the computer vision and navigation system. The project was meant to replicate a satellite communicating with a rover on the surface of Mars. Therefore a webcam was placed high above the work area. The computer vision and navigation system was created using Python and OpenCV, and communicated with the robot wirelessly via a TCP connection.

It was up to us to decide what we wanted our robot to do. We decided to show it navigating through an environment filled with obstacles, and also crossing a "Martian highway" to show its real time response to a changing environment

The Robot

The robot was a rolling robot, contained within a plastic ball. It was self balancing, with a pendulum used for turning. It relied on shifting its centre of gravity to make it roll.

As the ball was clear and the internal hardware was mostly constant, clusters of internal LEDs were used to determine the position and orientation of the robot.

Path planning

The vision system was capable of detecting the position and orientation of the robot, as well as obstacles and travel destinations. ArUco codes were used to mark these destinations. The Astar search algorithm was used to plan the routes that the robot could take to avoid the green obstacles.

This was done using various colour masks and pixelating the images.

Crossing a Martian highway

The Martian highway was projected onto the floor, using a projector. Cars, represented by ArUco codes, would enter the the two lanes from the left and right. The image processing software that I developed identifies the cars, determines their speed, and then predicts when they will enter the section of road that the robot has to cross. The robot then waits until the algorithm determines a safe time to cross, and then safely rolls across the road.The cars were spawned randomly and at different speeds, and the algorithm gained all the information about the environment from a webcam, next to the projector.
