Pedal powered bubble machine for a children's fair

My final year project was designing a pedal-powered bubble machine for a children's fair. The machine blows large bubbles with small bubbles contained within them.

Due to time constraints, the project was split into two sections - designing the bubble machine, and harnessing the power of the bike. After finishing university, and i finished integrating the two systems and took the machine to Ellafest, the children's fair that this was desigined for.

Initial research

Success in this project came from trying out lots of things, building quick prototypes to find out what works well and what doesn't. These protoypes were created to investigate the different ways of creating soap films for bubbles to be blown through.

Harnessing the power of the bike

The fair

The machine had some success, but a lot of hiccups on the day, but the fair was a really valuable learning experience for me. The machine itself was definately not robust enough, the gearbox in partucular was a big source of problems, however it was clear which parts were failing and which parts worked. I think it would not take too much work to create a robust version of the machine. It also really highlighted the differences between testing things in the lab and using them in the field.
